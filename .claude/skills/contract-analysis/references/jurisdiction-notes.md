# Jurisdiction-Specific Risk Notes

## Non-Compete Enforceability
- California: unenforceable → rate as INFO regardless of language
- New York: enforceable with reasonable limits
- England/Wales: blue-pencil doctrine — courts narrow, rarely void entirely
- Germany: requires compensation to be enforceable

## Force Majeure
- English law: no statutory FM, must be in contract → missing FM = HIGH
- French/German civil law: statutory FM exists → missing FM = MEDIUM

## Late Payment Interest
- EU Late Payment Directive: statutory interest applies → missing clause = lower risk in EU

## Data Privacy
- EU/UK GDPR applicable: missing Art. 28 DPA terms = HIGH
- US-only contracts: state-level only, lower regulatory risk

## Arbitration Cost Profile
- ICC (Paris): expensive, 18-24 month timeline
- AAA (domestic US): faster for <$1M disputes
- LCIA (London): strong global enforceability
